📊 Unit Trust Schemes (UTS)

Master how UTS works, from pooled investments to NAV calculations

What is UTS?

A pooled investment scheme where multiple investors combine money for professional management.

How UTS Works

💰 Investors Pool Money
→
📊 Fund Manager Invests
→
📈 Returns Distributed

🎯 Key Features

  • Diversification: Spreads risk across many investments
  • Liquidity: Sell units within 7 business days
  • Affordability: Start with as little as RM10
  • Professional Management: Expert fund managers
  • Transparency: Daily NAV published

💡 NAV - The Most Important Concept

Master this - it's on every exam!

NAV = Total Assets - Total Liabilities
NAV Per Unit = Total NAV ÷ Number of Units

✓ Real Example

Fund Assets: RM100 million

Fund Liabilities: RM10 million

Units in Circulation: 100 million

Calculation:

NAV = RM100M - RM10M = RM90M

NAV Per Unit = RM90M ÷ 100M = RM0.90

🏦 Private Retirement Schemes (PRS)

The 70/30 account structure is critical - understand it deeply

⭐ The 70/30 Account Structure

THIS IS THE MOST CRITICAL PRS CONCEPT!

Feature Sub-Account A (70%) Sub-Account B (30%)
Withdrawal ❌ Locked until 55 ✅ Once per year
Tax Penalty N/A 8% before retirement
Purposes Only special cases: death, disability Housing, healthcare, general

🎯 Default Fund Allocation by Age

If member doesn't choose, provider auto-allocates:

⚠️ 8% Tax Penalty Exemptions

Pre-retirement withdrawals from Sub-Account B face 8% tax UNLESS:

✓
Emigration: Permanent departure from Malaysia
✓
Death: With PPA authorization
✓
Disability: Permanent total disablement or serious disease
✓
Healthcare: Medical treatment for self/immediate family
✓
Housing: Buy, build, or repay house loan (NOT repairs!)

❌ Common Mistakes

  • House repairs/renovations do NOT qualify
  • Car loans do NOT qualify
  • Education for self (only if medical emergency)
  • General savings withdrawal without reason

✓ Scenario Example

Question: Ahmad is 48 years old with RM20,000 in Sub-Account B. He wants to renovate his house. Can he withdraw?

Answer: ❌ NO - House renovation does NOT qualify for exemption. He must pay 8% tax penalty on withdrawal.

But if he wanted to buy a new house instead, it WOULD be exempt!

🧮 Key Formulas & Calculations

Master these formulas - they appear on every exam in different forms

📐 1. NAV CALCULATIONS

NAV = Total Assets - Total Liabilities
NAV Per Unit = Total NAV ÷ Units in Circulation

✓ Practice: Calculate NAV Per Unit

Given: Assets = RM500M | Liabilities = RM50M | Units = 100M

NAV = RM500M - RM50M = RM450M

NAV Per Unit = RM450M ÷ 100M = RM4.50

💰 2. INVESTMENT CALCULATIONS

Working Money = Investment ÷ (1 + Service Charge %)
Units Purchased = Working Money ÷ NAV Per Unit

✓ Practice: Buy Units with Service Charge

Given: Investment = RM2,000 | Service Charge = 3% | NAV/Unit = RM1.50

Working Money = RM2,000 ÷ 1.03 = RM1,941.75

Units Purchased = RM1,941.75 ÷ RM1.50 = 1,294.50 units

📊 3. RETURN CALCULATIONS

Raw Return = (Ending Price - Beginning Price) ÷ Beginning Price × 100%
Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR): FV = PV × (1 + i)^n

✓ Practice: Calculate Raw Return

Given: Bought at RM1.00 | Sold at RM1.50 after 5 years

Raw Return = (1.50 - 1.00) ÷ 1.00 × 100% = 50% over 5 years

💎 4. SPECIAL FORMULAS

Rule of 72: 72 ÷ Interest Rate = Years to Double

✓ Practice: Use Rule of 72

Question: At 9% annual return, how many years to double investment?

Answer: 72 ÷ 9 = 8 years

Ex-NAV = Cum-NAV - Distribution Per Unit

✓ Practice: Distribution Impact

Given: Cum-NAV = RM2.00 | Distribution = RM0.20

Ex-NAV = RM2.00 - RM0.20 = RM1.80

Unit price drops by distribution amount - investor wealth unchanged!
