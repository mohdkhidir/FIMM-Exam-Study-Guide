## Guidelines on Advertising for Capital Market Products and Related Services

### Scope
These guidelines apply to the following persons who issue or authorise an advertisement or a promotional activity relating to a capital market product or capital market service:
- (a) An issuer of a capital market product
- (b) A person carrying out the regulated activity
- (c) A person who provides a capital market service
- (d) A person who operates or maintains a recognised market

### Minimum Standards Set Out in the Advertising Guidelines

Consultants are expected to observe the following:
- **a) Provide clear, fair and balanced information**
- **b) Treat investors fairly at all times**
- **c) Ensure advertisements fit their investors and can easily be understood**

## Consultants' Role in Investment Planning

Consultants should:
- Put investment planning in perspective by educating clients that investment decisions are preceded and based on investment strategies that are established to meet their financial goals within a certain time frame
- Educate clients regarding the risk, liquidity, tax and management characteristics of the investments to help them manage their investment risk and undertake the appropriate strategies necessary to achieve their financial goals
- Advise clients on the use of credit risks and the costs involved, investment decisions related to corporate plans, and investment strategies such as dollar cost averaging
- Discuss and recommend appropriate UTS and PRS products

---

## Specific Activities of the Consultants in Investment Planning

- Determine the client's financial objectives and order of importance
- Analyse the client's financial position and cash flow. Suggest changes to improve the client's cash position and analyse priorities, if necessary
- Determine the client's priorities for the allocation of financial resources among stated non-investment objectives, such as the amount of emergency funds and the acquisition of additional insurance
- Calculate the remaining assets that will be available to meet investment goals
- Gather and analyse the data necessary for developing the client's investment profile, including indications about the client's general disposition toward risks
- Determine the risk and return characteristics of the client's current investments
- State the financial assumptions
- Determine the adequacy of the client's resources for achieving stated investment goals and analyse the appropriateness of the client's current investment strategies
- If resources are insufficient, consider revising the allocation of resources, changing goals, or using investments with higher risks and yields that are acceptable by the client

## Income Distribution

### CUM-NAV per Unit (Cum-D)
**NAV per unit BEFORE distribution**

### EX-NAV per Unit (Ex-D)
**NAV per unit AFTER distribution**

### Formula:
**(EX ~ D) = (CUM ~ D) - Distribution**

---

### Example:
**ABC Growth Fund declares a distribution of 10 sen per unit. The Fund's NAV is RM180 million with 100 million units in circulation. Does the distribution make a unitholder wealthier?**

**NAV per Unit Before the Distribution:**
- RM180,000,000 / 100,000,000 units = RM1.80

**NAV per Unit After the Distribution:**
- (RM180,000,000 - RM10,000,000) / 100,000,000 units = RM1.70

**Unit price will be adjusted lower**

---

## Example: Case Study

**A unit holder in UTS, after receiving a distribution, would find that the NAV of his or her investment falls by the amount of that distribution.**

**The following table shows the unit holder's position before and after the distribution payment assuming the unit holder owns 1,000 units in the UTS:**

### Before Distribution Payment:
- NAV for 1,000 units = 1,000 units × RM1.80 per unit = RM1,800

### After Distribution Payment:
- NAV for 1,000 units = 1,000 units × RM1.70 per unit = RM1,700
- Distribution received = 1,000 units × 10 sen per unit = RM100

**Explain to the investor that the unit holder's position before and after distribution is the same.**

## Unit Split

### 1:3 Split
**For every 3 units I hold, I get 1 additional unit.**
**End Result: 4 Units**

### 1:10 Split
**For every 10 units I hold, I get 1 additional unit.**
**End Result: 11 Units**

### ⭐ To Reward Existing Investors with More Units
**Reduce the stated selling and repurchase prices & maintain a lower unit price (to attract more investors).**

---

## Unit Split Formula

**Units = (Units before split) × Original Units / Units After Split**

**NAV per Unit = (NAV per Unit before split) × Units After Split / Original Units**

### Example 1: Unit Split 1:2
**Ratio = 1:2**

**Units = 3/2 × Original Units = Units After Split**

**NAV per Unit = 3/2 × NAV per Unit = NAV per Unit after split**

### Example 2: Unit Split 1:4
**An investor has 100 units with an NAV per Unit of RM 0.25**

**The UTMC declares a Unit Split of 1:4**

| Item | Before Unit Split | After Unit Split |
|------|------------------|-----------------|
| Units | 100 Units | 5/4 × 100 = 125 units |
| NAV per Unit | RM 0.25 | 4/5 × 0.25 = RM 0.20 |
| NAV | 100 × 0.25 = RM 25 | 125 × 0.20 = RM 25 |

## Raw Return (Total Return)

**Raw Return = (End Price – Begin Price) / Begin Price × 100%**

### Example:
**5 Years (2015 - 2020)**

**Begin Price (RM 1) → End Price (RM 1.50)**

**Raw Return = (RM 1.50 – RM 1) / RM 1 × 100% = 50%**

**Raw Return (or Total Return) over 5 years = 50%**

## Compound Annual Growth Rate

**Measurement of an investment's annual growth rate over time, with compounding taken into account**

### Formula:
**FV = PV (1 + i)^n**

Where:
- **FV** = Future Value
- **PV** = Present Value
- **i** = Rate (compound annual growth rate)
- **n** = Years

---

## Compound Annual Growth Rate (Case Study)

**A UTS produced a 75% total return over a period of 5 years.**

## Rule of 72 ⭐

**Used when an investment value is double, with given rate or time.**

### Formula:
**72 / Years = Interest Rate**

**72 / Interest Rate = Years**

### Examples:

**An investor plans to double his investment value in 10 years' time, what is the required rate of return per annum?**

**72 / 10 = 7.2%**

**With an interest rate of 8% per annum, how long does it take to double an investment?**

**72 / 8% = 9 Years**

---

## Rule of 72 (Practice)

**Case Study:**
**Aaron invested RM130,000 and wants it to grow to RM260,000 within 14.4 years. What rate of return does he need to achieve this goal?**

**72 / 14.4 = 5%**

## Management Expense Ratio (MER)

**To assess previous year's cost of holding units in UTS.**

**Reducing MER indicates that the UTMC is managing the total fees & expenses MORE EFFICIENTLY given the fund size**

### Formula:
**MER = (Fees + Recovered Expenses) / Average Fund Size × 100%**

### We Can Use MER to Compare:
- Past vs prospective returns
- MER of competitor UTS
- Opportunity cost

**Note: The MER will have an impact on the fund's Effective Rate of Return**

## UTS & Investment-Linked Funds vs PRS

| Category | Unit Trust or Investment-Linked Funds | Private Retirement Scheme (PRS) |
|----------|------|---------|
| **Core Funds** | No core funds available. Offers various funds based on demand. | Provides 3 core funds: Conservative, Moderate, and Growth. |
| **Default Option** | No default option; investors must choose a fund. | Available for members who do not make a fund selection, based on age. |
| **Withdrawal** | No restriction on withdrawal | **Sub-account A:** Locked until retirement or special cases (death, disability, etc.). **Sub-account B:** Withdrawals allowed with conditions. |
| **Portability** | Need to sell first and use the proceed of withdrawal to buy from another fund house with sales charge. | Able to transfer investment from existing PRS provider to another. |

## Important Formula

### NAV Formula
**NAV = Total Assets – Total Liabilities**
**NAV per Unit = NAV / No. of Units**

### Working Money
**Working Money = Capital / (1 + Service Charge)**

### Number of Units
**No of units = Working Money ÷ NAV per unit**

### Redemption
**Redemption = No of units x NAV per unit**

### Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA)
**DCA = Total Investment ÷ No of units**

### Raw Return
**Raw Return = (End Value – Begin Value) / Begin Value × 100%**

### Compound Annual Return
**FV = PV (1 + i)^n**

### Unit Split 1:4
**Unit after split = (Units before split) × 5/4**
**NAV / U after split = (NAV / U before split) × 4/5**

### Ex-D = CUM-D – Distribution
**EX~D / = NAV CUM~D / NAV – Distribution**

### Rule of 72
**72 / Interest = Years**
**72 / Years = Interest**

### MER
**MER = (Expenses / Average Fund Size) × 100%**

### Loan Financing
**Loan Financing = 67%; Own Capital = 33%**
