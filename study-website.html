Unit Trust Schemes (UTS) Fundamentals

Master the basics of how Unit Trust Schemes work, from NAV calculations to investor protections.

What is a Unit Trust Scheme?

A Unit Trust Scheme is a pooled investment vehicle where multiple investors combine their money to be professionally managed. Each investor owns "units" that represent their share of the fund.

UTS Structure Overview

💰 Investors Pool Money
→
📊 Fund Manager Invests
→
📈 Returns Distributed

🎯 Key Characteristics

  • Diversification: Professional management spreads risk across many investments
  • Liquidity: Units can be bought/sold within 7 business days
  • Affordability: Start investing with as little as RM10
  • Professional Management: Experienced fund managers make investment decisions
  • Transparency: Daily unit pricing (NAV) is published

💡 NAV (Net Asset Value) Calculations

NAV is the fundamental concept in UTS - it determines unit prices and is used in all transactions.

NAV Formula:

NAV = Total Assets - Total Liabilities

NAV Per Unit:

NAV Per Unit = Total NAV / Number of Units in Circulation

🧮 Example Calculation

If a fund has:

  • Total Assets: RM 100 million
  • Total Liabilities: RM 10 million
  • Units in Circulation: 100 million

Then: NAV = RM90 million ÷ 100 million units = RM 0.90 per unit

📊 Types of UTS Funds

Fund Type Composition Risk Level Best For
Equity Funds Stocks & Shares High Long-term growth
Fixed Income Bonds & Debt Low-Moderate Regular income
Mixed Asset Stocks, Bonds, Cash Moderate Balanced portfolio
Money Market Short-term securities Very Low Liquidity & safety

💸 Fees & Charges

⚡ Direct Charges

Paid directly by investors:

  • Service Charge (entry fee)
  • Exit/Repurchase Fee
  • Switching Fee
  • Transfer Fee

📊 Indirect Charges

Deducted from fund value:

  • Annual Management Fee
  • Annual Trustee Fee
  • Fund Operations Expenses

Working Money Calculation:

Working Money = Investment Amount ÷ (1 + Service Charge)

Example: RM1,000 investment with 5% charge = RM1,000 ÷ 1.05 = RM952.38

Private Retirement Schemes (PRS) Complete Guide

Understand the unique features of PRS, withdrawal rules, and the 70/30 account structure that defines retirement savings in Malaysia.

What is a PRS?

A Private Retirement Scheme is a voluntary, long-term savings scheme designed to supplement mandatory retirement contributions and provide additional retirement income.

Why PRS Matters

💰 Extra Savings
→
💵 Tax Deduction
→
🏖️ Retirement Security

🎯 Key Benefits

  • Tax Incentive: Up to RM3,000 per year tax deduction (extended to 2030)
  • Flexible Contributions: No fixed amount or schedule required
  • Professional Management: Access to diverse investment options
  • Creditor Protection: Protected from judgment debt
  • Economic Growth: Provides long-term capital for economy

🏦 The 70/30 Account Structure ⭐ CRITICAL

This is one of the most important concepts for your exam!

Aspect Sub-Account A (70%) Sub-Account B (30%)
Withdrawal ❌ Locked until retirement ✅ Flexible withdrawal
Exceptions Only special cases: death, disability Housing, healthcare, general purposes
Frequency N/A Once per year
Tax Penalty N/A 8% tax penalty on pre-retirement
Retirement Age 55 years (or SC-specified age) 55 years

⚠️ Important: 8% Tax Penalty Exemptions

Pre-retirement withdrawals from Sub-Account B are NOT subject to 8% penalty if:

  • Permanent departure from Malaysia (emigration)
  • Death (with PPA authorization)
  • Permanent total disablement or serious disease
  • Healthcare for self/immediate family
  • Housing (buy, build, repay loan)

🎯 Default Fund Allocation

If members don't choose a specific fund, PRS Provider automatically allocates based on age:

Age Group Default Fund Investment Focus Risk Level
Under 45 Growth Fund Capital appreciation High
45-55 Moderate Fund Growth + Income Moderate
55+ Conservative Fund Income generation Low

💡 Remember

  • Provider must notify 1 month before automatic switch
  • Switches happen over 5-year period in equal proportions
  • Members can still choose their own funds at any time

Regulatory Framework & Code of Ethics

Understand the regulatory environment, the Code of Ethics, and your obligations as a financial consultant.

🏛️ Main Regulatory Authorities

Securities Commission Malaysia (SC)

  • Primary regulator for capital markets
  • Approves UTS and PRS schemes
  • Sets guidelines and regulations
  • Enforces market conduct standards
  • Issues licenses and permits

FIMM (Self-Regulatory)

  • Registers consultants/distributors
  • Enforces Code of Ethics
  • Investigates complaints
  • Maintains professional standards
  • Disciplinary proceedings

FMOS (Dispute Resolution)

  • Handles investor complaints
  • Independent & impartial
  • Covers securities & funds
  • Protects consumers
  • Timely resolutions

BNM (Bank Negara)

  • Monetary authority
  • Banking regulation
  • Custodian oversight
  • Liquidity management
  • Financial stability

⭐ Code of Ethics - 4 Core Principles

These are essential - memorize them!

1️⃣ Honesty & Integrity

  • Act with honesty, integrity, dignity
  • Professional and ethical conduct
  • Transparent in all dealings

2️⃣ Professionalism

  • Prompt and efficient service
  • Due care and diligence
  • Continuous improvement

3️⃣ Conflict of Interest

  • Disclose conflicts in writing
  • Prioritize investor interests
  • Avoid real/potential conflicts

4️⃣ Disclosure

  • Clear, balanced information
  • Consistent with regulatory docs
  • Appropriate disclaimers

⚠️ Prohibited Activities

  • ❌ Guaranteeing returns
  • ❌ Misrepresenting qualifications
  • ❌ Making unfounded claims
  • ❌ Aggressive or misleading selling
  • ❌ Concealing material information
  • ❌ Undisclosed conflicts of interest

🔑 Consultant Registration Requirements

✓
Age: At least 21 years old
✓
Qualification: Minimum 3 credits in any SPM subjects
✓
FIMM Exam: Must pass (unless exempted)
✓
Fit & Proper Test: Honesty, competence, financial soundness

⚡ Critical: 3+ Year Absence Rule

If you leave the industry for more than 3 years, you must sit for FIMM examination again before re-registering as a consultant.

Key Formulas & Calculations

Master the essential mathematical formulas that appear frequently in examinations.

📐 Basic NAV Calculations

NAV Formula

NAV = Total Assets - Total Liabilities

NAV Per Unit

NAV Per Unit = Total NAV ÷ Number of Units in Circulation

Unit Price (Same as NAV Per Unit)

Unit Price = Net Assets ÷ Units in Circulation

💰 Investment Calculations

Working Money (Amount Actually Invested)

Working Money = Investment ÷ (1 + Service Charge%)

Example: RM1,000 with 5% charge = RM1,000 ÷ 1.05 = RM952.38

Number of Units Purchased

No. of Units = Working Money ÷ NAV Per Unit

Redemption Proceeds

Redemption = No. of Units × NAV Per Unit

Dollar Cost Averaging

Average Cost/Unit = Total Invested ÷ Total Units Purchased

📊 Performance Metrics

Raw Return (Total Return)

Raw Return = (Ending Price - Beginning Price) ÷ Beginning Price × 100%

If price goes from RM1.00 to RM1.50 in 5 years: (1.50-1.00)/1.00 × 100 = 50% return

Compound Annual Growth Rate

FV = PV × (1 + i)^n

FV = Future Value | PV = Present Value | i = annual rate | n = years

Rule of 72 (Quick Estimation)

72 ÷ Interest Rate = Years to Double 72 ÷ Years = Required Interest Rate

At 8% return, investment doubles in 72÷8 = 9 years

Management Expense Ratio (MER)

MER = (Total Expenses ÷ Average Fund Size) × 100%

Shows the annual cost of holding units as percentage of fund size

🔄 Distribution & Unit Operations

Income Distribution

Ex-NAV = Cum-NAV - Distribution Per Unit

After distribution, NAV per unit is reduced by the amount distributed

Unit Split Impact (1:4 Example)

Units After = Units Before × 5÷4 NAV Per Unit After = NAV Per Unit Before × 4÷5

Total NAV remains unchanged; units increase, price per unit decreases

Loan Financing Ratio

Loan Amount = 67% of Investment Own Capital = 33% of Investment

Practice Questions & Answers

Test your knowledge with these sample questions covering all major topics. Click on any question to reveal the answer and explanation.

1
What is NAV per unit if a fund has Total NAV of RM100 million with 50 million units in circulation?
▼
Calculation:

NAV per Unit = RM100 million ÷ 50 million units = RM2.00

Explanation: NAV per unit is calculated by dividing total NAV by the number of units in circulation. This represents the price at which units are bought and sold.
2
An investor invests RM5,000 in a fund with a 3% service charge. What is the working money?
▼
Calculation:

Working Money = RM5,000 ÷ (1 + 0.03) = RM5,000 ÷ 1.03 = RM4,854.37

Service Charge = RM5,000 - RM4,854.37 = RM145.63

Explanation: The service charge is deducted from the investment amount. Working money is what actually goes into purchasing units. The remaining amount goes to fees.
3
What percentage of PRS contributions go to Sub-Account B, and when can withdrawals be made?
▼
Answer:

30% goes to Sub-Account B

Withdrawals can be made once per year for:

  • Housing purposes
  • Healthcare purposes
  • General purposes

Subject to: 8% tax penalty before retirement age (unless exempted)

Explanation: The 70/30 split is a fundamental aspect of PRS. Sub-Account A (70%) is locked until retirement, while Sub-Account B (30%) provides flexibility for specific needs, subject to tax penalties for pre-retirement withdrawals.
4
What are the 4 core principles of FIMM's Code of Ethics?
▼
The 4 Core Principles are:
  1. Honesty & Integrity - Act professionally and conduct with dignity
  2. Professionalism - Provide prompt, efficient service with due diligence
  3. Conflict of Interest - Disclose conflicts and prioritize investor interests
  4. Disclosure of Information - Provide clear, balanced, transparent information
Explanation: These four principles are the foundation of FIMM's Code of Ethics. They apply to all consultants and distributors. You must memorize these for the examination.
5
A UTS fund declares a distribution of 10 sen per unit. After the distribution, how does the unit price change?
▼
Answer:

The unit price DECREASES by 10 sen

Formula: Ex-NAV = Cum-NAV - Distribution

Example: If Cum-NAV is RM1.80 and distribution is RM0.10

Ex-NAV = RM1.80 - RM0.10 = RM1.70

Explanation: Distribution paid to unit holders reduces the fund's NAV. This is not "free money" — the unit holder receives a distribution but the unit price drops by the same amount. The total value remains the same for unit holders who received the distribution.
6
What is the default fund allocation for a PRS member aged 48 years old who didn't choose any fund?
▼
Answer:

Moderate Fund

Default Allocation Rules:

  • <45 years → Growth Fund
  • 45-55 years → Moderate Fund (48 falls here)
  • >55 years → Conservative Fund
Explanation: Moderate Fund balances growth with income generation, appropriate for mid-career investors approaching retirement. The provider must notify the member 1 month before automatic switching occurs.
7
What is the first step in the 6-step financial planning process?
▼
Answer:

Step 1: Establish Client-Planner Relationship

This involves:

  • Defining services to be provided
  • Clarifying responsibilities
  • Discussing costs and fees
  • Setting expectations
Explanation: Before gathering any data or making recommendations, you must establish trust and clear understanding with the client about the scope and cost of the planning engagement. This foundational step prevents misunderstandings later.
8
If an investment grows from RM100,000 to RM175,000 in 5 years, what is the approximate compound annual growth rate (using Rule of 72)?
▼
Calculation:

This is 75% total return (growth of RM75,000)

This means investment went from 1× to 1.75×

Using CAGR formula: 1.75 = (1 + i)^5

Solving: i ≈ 11.84% CAGR

Rule of 72 check: 72 ÷ 11.84% ≈ 6 years to double (close to our 5-year 75% return)

Explanation: CAGR shows the annualized growth rate including compounding effects. It's more meaningful than average return because it accounts for the time value of money. Always distinguish between total return (75% over 5 years) and annual rate (11.84%).
9
Which of the 8% PRS tax penalty exemptions apply to paying for house repairs?
▼
Answer:

NO - House repairs do NOT qualify

Housing exemptions DO include:

  • ✓ Buy a house (purchase)
  • ✓ Build a house (construction)
  • ✓ Reduce/redeem housing loan
  • ✗ Renovate/repair existing house

Healthcare exemptions: For self or immediate family members' medical/health purposes (listed conditions only)

Explanation: The exemptions are specifically for acquiring housing or healthcare treatment, not for maintenance or non-essential improvements. This is a common trap question on exams — students often assume all housing-related expenses qualify.
10
A consultant must disclose conflicts of interest. What is the required method?
▼
Answer:

Disclose conflicts of interest in WRITING to the investor

Requirements:

  • Must be written (not verbal)
  • Must be given to investor
  • Must clearly state the nature of conflict
  • Must allow investor to make informed decision
Explanation: Written disclosure creates a record and ensures the investor has full information before making decisions. This is part of the \"Disclosure of Information\" core principle — transparency protects both parties and upholds professional integrity.
